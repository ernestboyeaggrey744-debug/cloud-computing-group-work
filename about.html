The Ghana Shippers' Authority (GSA) was established in 1974 with a mandate to protect and promote the interests of Ghanaian shippers (exporters and importers) in relation to international trade and transport logistics. The Authority serves as an advocate for shippers, providing guidance and support to enhance their competitiveness and reduce shipping costs.

Over the years, GSA has played a critical role in developing Ghana‚Äôs maritime and shipping sector by improving port infrastructure, ensuring fair shipping practices, and facilitating smooth cargo movement. The Authority collaborates with key stakeholders including government agencies, port operators, and international shipping lines to create a reliable and efficient shipping environment for Ghanaian businesses.
